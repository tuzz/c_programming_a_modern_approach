The 'future library directions' section of the C99 standard can be found here:
https://rgambord.github.io/c99-doc/sections/7/26/index.html

All names desribed below are reserved, regardless of which headers are included.

## complex.h

These names are reserved and also with 'f' or 'l' suffixes:
- cerf
- cexpm1
- clog2
- cerfc
- clog10
- clgamma
- cexp2
- clog1p
- ctgamma

## ctype.h

Functions that begin with 'is' or 'to' followed by a lowercase letter are reserved.

## errno.h

Macros that begin with 'E' followed by a digit or uppercase letter are reserved.

## inttypes.h

Macros that begin with 'PRI' or 'SCN' followed by a lowercase letter or 'X' are reserved.

## locale.h

Macros that begin with 'LC_' followed by an uppercase letter are reserved.

## signal.h

Macros that begin with 'SIG' or 'SIG_' and an uppercase letter are reserved.

## stdint.h

Typedefs that begin with 'int' or 'uint' and ending with '_t' are reserved.

## stdio.h

Conversion specifiers and length modifiers for lowercase letters are reserved for fprintf and fscanf.

## stdlib.h

Functions that begin with 'str' followed by a lowercase letter are reserved.

## string.h

Functions that begin with 'str', 'mem' or 'wcs' followed by a lowercase letter are reserved.

## wchar.h

Functions that begin with 'wcs' followed by a lowercase letter are reserved.

Conversion specifiers and length modifiers for lowercsae letters are reserved for fwprintf and fwscanf.

## wctype.h

Functions that begin with 'is' or 'to' followed by a lowercase letter are reserved.
